About Me

I am a PhD student in economics at the Institute for International Economic Studies (IIES) in Stockholm. My research focuses on macroeconomics and productivity, exploring the dynamics of economic growth and development.

My approach involves developing macroeconomic models that are motivated and disciplined by Swedish administrative employer-employee data. I also have an affinity for coding and applying machine learning methods to economics and have done work on reinforcement learning-based pricing algorithms.

Research

Macroeconomic Theory

Research on theoretical models for economic growth and productivity.

Productivity Growth

Analyzing the impact of technology diffusion on long-term economic growth.
